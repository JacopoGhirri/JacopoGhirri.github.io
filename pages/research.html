What I Work On

Deep Learning for CO2 Emission Forecasting

Developed LSTM and Transformer-based forecasting pipelines to predict short-term CO2 emissions across EU countries and sectors. Handles hundreds of correlated drivers — energy, economic, and transition indicators — to assess whether current trends align with 2030 reduction targets. Built end-to-end in Python (PyTorch, Pandas) on large-scale panel data from Eurostat, IEA, and EDGAR.

Skills: Deep learning, time-series forecasting, feature engineering, large-scale data processing

Generative AI for Scenario Exploration (GFlowNets)

At MILA, I developed generative models (GFlowNets) to explore the space of feasible climate mitigation strategies within macroeconomic simulators. This moves beyond single-optimum solutions to produce diverse, robust scenario sets — a methodology transferable to portfolio optimization, stress testing, and strategic planning.

Skills: Generative models, PyTorch, stochastic sampling, robust optimization

Robust Decision-Making under Model Uncertainty

Designed frameworks for policy evaluation when the underlying model may be misspecified — using multiplier preferences and statistical divergence measures. Directly applicable to model risk management, regulatory stress testing, and any domain where decisions must be robust to unknown unknowns.

Skills: Statistical modeling, uncertainty quantification, robust decision making, sensitivity analysis
