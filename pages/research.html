Short term CO2 emission forecasts in the EU

A 55% reduction in CO2 emissions by 2030 is an ambitious goal, and disruptive trends are making it ever more challenging to forecast whether we are on track or not.
Traditional forecasting methods struggle in modeling a high amount of highly correlated variables. I am developing a Deep Learning solution capable of handling many emission drivers, and transition indicators, across the sectors of EU countries.
It will hence be possible to assess how traditionally computed scenarios compare with data-driven trend extrapolations.

A new generation of Climate Scenarios

Integrates Assessment Models have long been pivotal in understanding the impacts, and shaping climate policies. They however face the limitations posed by the optimization framework often central to their architecture.
GFlowNets are a promising generative model architecture, and can help in exploring the wider space of mitigation strategies. Moving beyond an optimization framework can yield a more diverse set of feasible mitigation strategies, and assess the robustness of optimal solutions.
