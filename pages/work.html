Projects

Multivariate Deep Learning Forecasting for EU Emissions

Methods: Deep learning, multivariate time series, high-dimensional correlated features

Developing a deep learning pipeline to produce short-term CO2 emission forecasts across sectors and EU countries. The model handles a large number of highly correlated drivers and transition indicators where traditional econometric methods struggle. The goal is to benchmark data-driven trend extrapolations against scenario-based projections, giving policymakers an independent signal on whether emission targets are on track.

Generative Models for Combinatorial Policy Exploration

Methods: GFlowNets, generative modeling, constrained combinatorial optimization

Built at MILA, this project uses GFlowNets — a class of generative models that sample diverse, high-quality solutions proportional to a reward — to explore the space of climate mitigation strategies. Rather than finding a single optimum, the approach generates a diverse set of feasible strategies under constraints, enabling robustness analysis of any given solution. The method generalizes to any domain where you need diverse near-optimal solutions over discrete, structured spaces.

Robust Decision-Making under Model Misspecification

Methods: Bayesian inference, robust optimization, uncertainty quantification

Developed a framework for making policy decisions when the underlying models may be wrong. Using techniques from robust statistics and decision theory, the approach quantifies how sensitive optimal decisions are to model assumptions and identifies strategies that perform well across a range of plausible models. Directly applicable to any high-stakes forecasting or planning problem where model risk matters — from portfolio construction to supply chain optimization.
